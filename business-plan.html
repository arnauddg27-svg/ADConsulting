Executive Summary

The Opportunity

Central Florida’s residential construction market continues to grow, yet most small-to-midsize home builders (under 200 homes per year) operate without dedicated analytics, repeatable operating systems, or the reporting infrastructure needed to make confident decisions at scale. Leadership teams rely on spreadsheets, tribal knowledge, and ad-hoc data pulls that take hours to assemble and are often stale by the time they arrive.

A.D. Homes & Consulting bridges this gap. The practice delivers Power BI dashboards, financial modeling, and ERP process improvement directly to builders who have outgrown manual reporting but are not large enough to justify a full-time analytics hire or Big-4 consulting engagement.

Every engagement is handled personally by Arnaud Durand — an MBA-trained operations professional with 6+ years of builder-side experience managing 1,200+ lots and 1,000+ home closings across Central Florida.

Target Market

Who This Practice Serves

The ideal client is a Central Florida home builder or land developer closing 20 – 200 homes per year. These firms have real operating complexity — multiple communities, dozens of active jobs, vendor networks, and financing requirements — but lack the internal staff or tools to monitor it all in a structured way.

Primary Audience

  • Production home builders (20 – 200 closings/year)
  • Infill and spec builders scaling from 10+ homes
  • Land developers managing lot takedowns
  • Regional builders expanding into new communities

Common Pain Points

  • No weekly operating view — data is scattered across ERP, email, and spreadsheets
  • Job cost reports take hours to compile manually
  • Land deals underwritten on gut feel, not models
  • ERP purchased but barely configured or adopted
  • No repeatable process for schedule, purchasing, or permitting

Geographic focus: Orange, Osceola, Seminole, Lake, and Polk counties — the core of the Orlando MSA’s new-home activity. Key sub-markets include Winter Garden, Clermont, Apopka, Kissimmee, St. Cloud, and Davenport.

Competitive Position

Why A.D. Homes & Consulting

What Sets This Practice Apart

  • Builder-side experience. Not a general consultant learning construction — 6 years inside an active builder managing real lots, real schedules, and real vendor issues
  • One person, no layers. The person who scopes the work is the person who builds the dashboard and trains the team
  • Flat-fee, scoped projects. No hourly billing surprises or open-ended retainers
  • Implementation, not advice. Deliverables are working tools — dashboards, models, templates — not slide decks

Competitive Landscape

  • Big-4 / national firms: Priced at $300–500/hr, minimum $50K+ engagements. Overkill for builders under 200 homes
  • ERP vendors (BuildPro, BRIX): Sell software, not operational consulting. Configuration is left to the builder
  • Freelance BI developers: Build dashboards but lack construction domain knowledge
  • In-house hire: A full-time ops analyst costs $70K–$100K+ loaded. Most small builders can’t justify the headcount

Go-to-Market Strategy

How the Practice Acquires Clients

Primary Channels

  • Direct outreach. Personal network from 6+ years in Central FL building industry — builder owners, land developers, lenders, title companies
  • Referral network. ERP vendors, accountants, and real estate attorneys who serve the same builder segment
  • Website & sample dashboard. Interactive demo at adhomesconsulting.com shows capabilities before the first call
  • Local builder associations. Greater Orlando Builders Association (GOBA), HBA events, NAHB networking

Sales Process

  • Step 1: Free 30-minute discovery call — understand the operation, identify the constraint
  • Step 2: Written scope + flat-fee proposal within 48 hours
  • Step 3: Kick off with 50% deposit. Work begins within 1 week of signed agreement
  • Step 4: Deliver, train, collect final payment
  • Step 5: Offer retainer for ongoing support. Convert project clients to recurring revenue

The conversion funnel is short by design. Builders are busy and skeptical of long sales cycles. The free discovery call, fast turnaround on proposals, and the interactive sample dashboard on the website reduce friction and build credibility before a contract is signed.
